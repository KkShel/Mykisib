Introduction!

Mykisib: An Unfamiliar Odyssey

"The planet Uhust is home to Mykse-Binheso, a travelling Seer and fan of local theatre. Mykse has had a vision of vibrant worlds that exist light-years beyond his star system, and he plans to visit them. All he needs is to find the alien ship that he saw landing in the woods."

Genres and keywords:
Science-fiction, Fantasy, Queer Themes, Introspection, Religion, Speculative Biology1, Hero’s Journey2, Capitalism3, Consumerism4, Classism5, Social Media, Celebrity Culture6, Bodily Autonomy7, Ableism8

This project stemmed from my interest in the alien worlds and cultures of Star Wars. I created a few original characters and ended up with several groups with their own narratives. Eventually, I abandoned the parts that were not originally mine, and Mykisib came into being. I wanted to create alien worlds that did not fall into the tropes of orientalism or euro-centrism, a story that celebrated and explored a diverse setting with respect and care.

Developing this story and setting also serves as a means for me to examine and interpret political and cultural issues that interest me. Sexuality and gender identity, the development of religions and how individuals interpret their beliefs, interpretations on the idea of biological race and how it interacts with culture, how people interact within different class and economic systems, and how worlds develop and change over time; what’s left of the past and how it influences the future. If Mykisib ever gains an audience, I hope that it shows them a thoughtful perspective on life on Earth through a creative, unique, and considerate lens.

I am frequently caught in the midst of a dilemma: I want to learn, but I refuse to read. As a result, I spend a lot of my time pondering and mulling thoughts over instead of finding answers. Developing this work serves as my outlet.

While I do take inspiration from some media, there are many works in my favourite genres that I have not seen or read, so I constantly fear that I am accidentally reinventing the wheel. What I’m doing may have been done before, better than I could ever hope to achieve, and I could be completely unaware. Luckily, this is a hobby for my own amusement. It can be as unoriginal or unnecessarily complicated as I care to make it.
